What Is "View from the Cheap Seats"?

The title says it all. I'm not a senator. I'm not a pundit with a green room and a makeup artist. I'm watching from the back of the arena, far enough away that I don't have perfect information — and I'm honest about that.

What I do have is the ability to look at what's actually being said and ask: is that true? Is that consistent? Or is that just what someone needs to say right now to keep their audience happy?

Some facts are genuinely contested — whether there's a God, what the right abortion policy is, how to balance economic growth against inequality. Reasonable people disagree on those, and I try to hold that complexity. But other things aren't contested. A tariff is a tax paid by the importer, which typically means the domestic consumer. That's not political opinion — that's economics. When a politician tells you otherwise, they're not sharing a different perspective. They're telling you something untrue in order to get you to behave the way they want.

That's what bothers me. And that's what I write about.

Who I Am

I grew up in Wasco, California — a small farming town in the San Joaquin Valley. I went to the United States Naval Academy in Annapolis, graduated with a degree in mathematics in 1980, and spent eight years in the Navy.

After the service I joined the civilian world, earned an M.S. in Management from Purdue University and an MBA in International Business from NEOMA Business School in Rouen, France. I've been running my own consulting company — Shiftwork Solutions — for over 35 years, helping manufacturers, distributors, and utilities redesign their shift schedules. I've worked with more than 300 organizations across North America and beyond.

I grew up Republican and was a registered Republican well into my forties. What changed me wasn't ideology — it was watching politicians I trusted say things I knew were false in the run-up to the Iraq War. I became an independent. I don't see myself as a liberal. I see myself as progressive — someone who believes in facts, in institutions that are worth defending, and in the value of changing your mind when the evidence changes.

I live in Marin County, California, with my wife Erika and our Old English Sheepdog.

How These Posts Are Made

I write these posts. Every idea, every argument, every word choice starts with me. But before anything goes up, I run each draft through a panel of AI systems — including models from Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, and others — as part of what I call the Swarm review process. They poke holes. They surface blind spots. They flag logical inconsistencies.

Then I decide what to do with that feedback.

I think that's more honest than pretending AI doesn't exist — or pretending that having a human byline means the thinking was done in a vacuum. The words are mine. The arguments are mine. The AI helps me make sure I'm not embarrassing myself.

A Note on Comments

You're welcome here even if you disagree with everything I write. I mean that. The only rule is civility. Attack the argument, not the person — including me. I genuinely read every comment, and I've been wrong before. If you can show me where, I'll say so.
